Certification Journey

Databricks Data Engineer Associate Journey

A hands-on path from production data platforms to the Lakehouse certification, covering PySpark, Delta Lake, and real-world exam strategy.

Why This Certification

As a Data Platform Engineer working daily with PySpark, Delta Lake, and Databricks, I wanted a credential that validates production-grade skills, not just theory. The Databricks Data Engineer Associate exam focuses on the Lakehouse Platform, ETL with Apache Spark, Delta Lake operations, and production workflows — the exact stack I use at Accenture.

What the Exam Covers

  • Databricks Lakehouse Platform and workspace fundamentals
  • ETL with Apache Spark on Databricks
  • Delta Lake: tables, time travel, optimization, and governance
  • Relational entities and data modeling in the lakehouse
  • Production workloads, jobs, and orchestration

My Preparation Path

  1. Completed the Databricks Academy learning path and hands-on labs.
  2. Applied lakehouse concepts on real Accenture projects using PySpark, Delta Lake, and CI/CD pipelines.
  3. Built side projects to reinforce Delta Lake and Structured Streaming patterns.
  4. Took practice assessments to identify and close knowledge gaps.

Resources That Helped

  • Databricks Academy free learning plan
  • Official exam guide and sample questions
  • Hands-on Databricks Community Edition labs
  • Real-world production scenarios from my current role

Key Lessons

  • Practice Delta Lake operations — MERGE, OPTIMIZE, ZORDER, and time travel are exam-heavy.
  • Understand Spark internals — lazy evaluation, transformations vs actions, and cluster configs.
  • Know Unity Catalog basics — data governance is a recurring theme.
  • Time management matters — flag uncertain questions and review later.

What's Next

I am extending into the Databricks Data Engineer Professional and advanced streaming certifications, while applying these patterns to production data platforms.
