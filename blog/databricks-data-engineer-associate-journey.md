# My Databricks Data Engineer Associate Journey

## Why I Chose This Certification

As a **Data & AI Platform Engineer** working daily with **PySpark**, **Delta Lake**, and **Databricks**, I wanted a credential that validated my production-grade skills, not just theory. The **Databricks Data Engineer Associate** exam focuses on the **Lakehouse Platform**, **ETL with Apache Spark**, **Delta Lake operations**, and production workflows — exactly the stack I use at work.

## What the Exam Covers

- Databricks Lakehouse Platform and workspace fundamentals
- ETL with Apache Spark on Databricks
- Delta Lake: tables, time travel, optimization, and governance
- Relational entities and data modeling in the lakehouse
- Production workloads, jobs, and orchestration

## My Preparation Path

- Completed Databricks Academy learning path and hands-on labs
- Applied lakehouse concepts on real Accenture projects (PySpark, Delta Lake, CI/CD pipelines)
- Built side projects to reinforce Delta Lake and Structured Streaming patterns
- Took practice assessments to close knowledge gaps

## Resources That Helped

- [Databricks Academy](https://www.databricks.com/learn/training) free learning plan
- Official exam guide and sample questions
- Hands-on Databricks Community Edition labs
- Real-world production scenarios from my current role

## Key Lessons

1. **Practice Delta Lake operations** — `MERGE`, `OPTIMIZE`, `ZORDER`, and time travel are exam-heavy.
2. **Understand Spark internals** — lazy evaluation, transformations vs actions, and cluster configurations.
3. **Know Unity Catalog basics** — data governance is a recurring theme.
4. **Time management matters** — flag uncertain questions and review later.

I’m extending into the **Databricks Data Engineer Professional** and advanced streaming certifications, while applying these patterns to production data platforms.
