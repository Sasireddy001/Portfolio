## Comment strategy

### For your own posts
- Reply to **every comment within 2 hours** for the first 24 hours.
- Keep replies under 3 lines; ask a follow-up question to keep the thread alive.
- Pin a comment with the link to the portfolio or relevant repository after 1 hour.
- Tag 1–2 people in the comments only if they are directly relevant.

### On others' posts (warm-up and visibility)
- Spend 15 minutes before and after your own post engaging on #DataEngineering, #PySpark, #Databricks, #MLOps, #DataPlatform.
- Leave thoughtful comments, not emojis.
- Target 5 comments per day on weekdays, 2 per day on weekends.
- Comment on posts by recruiters, hiring managers, and data engineering influencers.

---

## Engagement strategy

### First hour (critical)
- Share the post in 2–3 relevant LinkedIn groups immediately after publishing.
- Send the post link to 3 trusted colleagues and ask them to comment or share if they find it useful.
- Respond to comments quickly to boost the algorithm.

### First 24 hours
- Check notifications every 2–3 hours.
- Repost or quote the best comment as a new post only if it gets strong engagement.
- Add a follow-up comment with a key insight or screenshot if the post is performing well.

### 3-day follow-up
- If a post gets >50 engagements, repost the core lesson in a slightly different format on Day 3.
- If a post gets >10 profile views, send connection requests to 3–5 viewers with a personalized note.

## Networking strategy

### Daily (weekdays)
- Send 3–5 connection requests to:
  - Hiring managers at product companies
  - Senior data engineers / staff engineers
  - Technical recruiters
  - Databricks / AWS / Microsoft employees
- Personalize each note with one shared interest or project.

### Weekly
- Accept all relevant connection requests.
- Engage with 2–3 posts from new connections.
- Message 2–3 existing connections to catch up or ask a question.

### Monthly
- Request 1–2 recommendations from managers or senior colleagues.
- Ask 3–5 people for skill endorsements.
- Review profile analytics and double down on the topics with highest engagement.
