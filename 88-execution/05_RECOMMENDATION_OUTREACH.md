# LinkedIn Recommendation & Endorsement Outreach Kit

**Goal:** Collect 3–5 LinkedIn recommendations and targeted skill endorsements from people who can speak to your data engineering work.

---

## Whom to ask

1. **Current or previous manager** — best for leadership, ownership, delivery.
2. **Technical lead or architect** — best for design, architecture, code quality.
3. **Senior engineer / staff engineer** — best for technical depth and collaboration.
4. **Close colleague / peer** — best for teamwork, reliability, mentoring.
5. **Client / product owner** — best for business impact and communication.

---

## How to ask

### Rules for every message
- Keep it under 150 words.
- Remind them of one specific project or result.
- Offer to write a draft for them.
- Make the request specific (role, skills, duration).
- Give them an easy out.

---

## 1. Manager

**Use when:** you reported to them directly and they saw your project outcomes.

```
Hi [Name],

I hope you are doing well. I am updating my LinkedIn profile to better reflect my data platform work, and I would really appreciate a recommendation from you.

If you are open to it, I can draft a short paragraph focused on [specific project, e.g., the Core Data Product platform / the Kafka → Delta pipeline / the testing initiative]. I would be happy for it to highlight ownership, delivery, and any business impact you observed.

No worries at all if you are busy — just let me know.

Thanks,
Sasidhar
```

**Follow-up draft you can send**

```
Suggested angle: "Sasidhar led the design and operation of our configuration-driven Core Data Product platform, covering 90+ production PySpark ETL jobs. He introduced pytest-based testing and modular job definitions that reduced deployment time and improved reliability. He consistently delivered clean, well-documented work and took ownership end-to-end."
```

```
Let me know if that works.

Thanks,
Sasidhar
```

**Follow-up draft you can send**

```
Suggested angle: "Sasidhar designed and implemented the architecture for our streaming data platform, including Kafka, PySpark, Delta Lake, and Terraform-based AWS/Azure infrastructure. His code was well-structured, tested, and production-ready. He is strong at turning complex requirements into scalable, maintainable data systems."
```

## Skill endorsements

After someone writes a recommendation, send a short follow-up asking for endorsements on specific skills:

```
Hi [Name],

Thanks again for the recommendation. If you have a moment, could you also endorse me for PySpark, Apache Kafka, Delta Lake, and Data Engineering on LinkedIn? Those are the skills I am targeting for my next role.

Appreciate it.
Sasidhar
```

## Professional references

For roles that ask for references, ask one manager, one senior engineer, and one colleague:

```
Hi [Name],

I am in the final stages of interview loops for Data Engineer / Data Platform Engineer roles. Would you be willing to be a professional reference if a recruiter reaches out?

It would likely be a 10–15 minute call about our work together on [project]. I can share the job description and key points I would like them to hear.

Thanks,
Sasidhar
```
